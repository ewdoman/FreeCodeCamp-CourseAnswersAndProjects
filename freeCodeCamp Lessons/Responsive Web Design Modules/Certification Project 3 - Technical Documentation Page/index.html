Introduction

Brownies are a beloved chocolate dessert that combines the richness of cake with the chewiness of a cookie.

In this guide, you'll learn how to make classic fudgy brownies from scratch using common pantry ingredients.

Ingredients

Here is a basic list of what you'll need:

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup (40g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
Instructions

Follow these steps to bake your brownies:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9 inch baking pan.
  2. Melt the butter and mix with sugar, eggs, and vanilla.
  3. Add cocoa, flour, salt, and baking powder. Stir until well combined.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly.
  5. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes. Let cool before cutting.
Tips & Tricks

Here is a pro-tip: Do not overbake!

  • Use high-quality cocoa for richer flavor.
  • Line the pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
  • Add chocolate chips or nuts for extra texture.
  • Chill the batter for 30 minutes before baking for a denser texture.
  • Dust the top with powdered sugar or drizzle with melted chocolate after baking for presentation.
  • Use room-temperature eggs for better emulsion and texture.
FAQ

Q: Can I use oil instead of butter?
A: Yes, but it will slightly change the flavor and texture.

Q: How do I make brownies chewier?
A: Use more brown sugar and a bit less flour for a chewier texture.

Q: Can I freeze brownies?
A: Yes, wrap them tightly and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Q: What type of cocoa powder should I use?
A: Natural unsweetened cocoa works well, but Dutch-processed cocoa adds a smoother flavor.

Q: Why did my brownies come out dry?
A: They were likely overbaked. Try checking them a few minutes earlier next time.
